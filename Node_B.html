NODE 2 HISTORICAL REVIEW: CLIMATE COLLAPSE

Timeframe: Common Era, years 2020–2035

During this period, human activities led to significant environmental degradation, resulting in catastrophic climate events and widespread ecological collapse.

Oceanic Fungal Proliferation

Rising sea temperatures facilitated the spread of harmful fungal pathogens, such as Aspergillus species, threatening marine ecosystems and human health.

Surface Temperature Increase

Global temperatures rose, leading to increased incidence of heat-related illnesses, including skin and respiratory diseases.

Forest Fires

Elevated temperatures and prolonged droughts resulted in frequent and intense wildfires, destroying vast forest areas and releasing significant carbon emissions.

Species Extinction

Habitat loss and climate stressors led to the extinction of numerous species, disrupting ecological balance.

Extreme Weather Events

Increased frequency of floods, hurricanes, and droughts displaced large populations and caused substantial loss of life.

Food Shortages

Climate-induced agricultural disruptions led to global food insecurity, speeding up malnutrition and famine.

Coral Reef Degradation

Ocean acidification and warming caused widespread coral bleaching, leading to the collapse of marine biodiversity, and a steep drop in oxygen levels.

ORIGIN

These compounded effects rendered Earth's environment hostile to human life, leading to societal collapse and the eventual collapse of human civilization.
